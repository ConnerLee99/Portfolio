How Copyright affects websites

Copyright affects what you can put on your website, whether that be text, images, videos or audio. All content on your website must be either copyright free, meaning no one owns the rights to said element. All content that is copyrighted must be used with consent from the owner. Or content used can fall under fair use, this is usually only for educational purposes. Any copyrighted content used without permission can result in you having to pay civil damages to the owner. Your website will meet legal requirements as long as it follows the WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility standard.

Accessibility

When making a website you need to always keep in mind accessibility. Common mistakes that affect this are as follows. Too low contrast, inappropriate Alt attributes, colour=drives instructions. Lack of keyboard accessibility, CAPTCHAs. All these and more must be taken into account. However you also need to make your website safe and also adhere to what you want it to look like. Thinks like CAPTCHAs are also needed for protection against bots.

File Types

There are a lot of different file types than you can use. And browsers now support more than ever, but it is important to know what to use for what occasion for example Logos are usually vector images, so they cal be resized and not become pixelated. AVI is usually what is used for video files with audio. JPG/JPEG is commonly used for photos and MIDI/MID is another.
